Rest day at Ax les Thermes

Saturday September 9, 2017

It started raining just as predicted and is quite cold so we were glad we were not riding. We accompanied our 'warm showers' host Yann to the local markets which were quite interesting but the rain intensified so we came back to Yann's home and Yann cooked us a delicious lunch followed by local bread and cheese. The best lunch we've had so far on our trip. It was a quiet afternoon with Blue fettling the brakes once more, this time with the aid of a bench grinder.

Yann tells us the road to Andorra is narrow and busy (lots of people go to Andorra to buy duty free goods). We are planning an relatively early start in the morning (for us) to hopefully beat the worst of the traffic. Ax les Thermes is at 740 m altitude and the pass is at a out 2200 m so there will be a bit of climbing. We had planned to do this over two days but because of the traffic we will try to do it in one. The rain is due to stop at 2 am.
